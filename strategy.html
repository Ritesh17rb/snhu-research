Act I: Priority Bets — Strategic Research Directions

To maximize SNHU's research impact and align with evolving academic landscapes, we recommend prioritizing investment in areas demonstrating strong growth, high citation potential, and existing institutional expertise. The analysis considers both historical performance and future projections (especially for 2025 and 2026), acknowledging that future figures represent forecasts rather than completed output.

1. Computer Science: The Citation Powerhouse with Sustained Growth

  • Data Points: Computer Science accounts for 616 papers, the second-highest overall, and dominates the top 20 most-cited papers (17 out of 20, including "Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition" with 217,861 citations). While there was a dip in 2021-2022, projections show a strong rebound (24 papers in 2025, 18 in 2026). Its subfields like Artificial Intelligence (250 papers), Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (161), and Computer Networks and Communications (62) are highly relevant and impactful. Topics like "Advanced Neural Network Applications" (49) and "Neural Networks and Applications" (27) demonstrate specific areas of strength.
  • Strategic Recommendation: SNHU should cement its position as a leader in applied Computer Science, particularly in areas like Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and their applications. This includes deep learning, computer vision, and potentially natural language processing (given the AI-assisted content generation paper). Investing in infrastructure, faculty recruitment, and graduate programs in these areas will leverage SNHU's significant past impact and current momentum. Focus on real-world applications relevant to industry and societal challenges.

2. Social Sciences: Foundational Strength and Consistent Performance

  • Data Points: With 708 papers, Social Sciences is SNHU's largest field by volume. It shows remarkably consistent output, with recent spikes (27 papers in 2023, 24 in 2024) and projected stability (23 papers in 2025). Key subfields like Sociology and Political Science (266), Education (176), and topics such as "Qualitative Research Methods and Applications" (37) highlight a robust foundation. One paper, "Three Approaches to Qualitative Content Analysis" (2005), is highly cited (43,185).
  • Strategic Recommendation: Leverage the consistent high volume and foundational strength in Social Sciences. SNHU should prioritize research that addresses contemporary societal issues, educational innovation (especially given SNHU's online learning model), and policy impacts. Encourage interdisciplinary collaborations with Psychology and Business to explore social behaviors, organizational dynamics, and educational efficacy. Promote qualitative and mixed-methods research, building on existing strengths.

  • Data Insight: The research output has seen fluctuations, with a notable decline in papers per year from 2016-2023. This could be due to various factors, including faculty turnover or shifts in research focus.
  • Recommendation: Implement robust mentorship programs for early-career researchers, pairing them with highly productive and cited senior faculty. Offer workshops on grant writing, research design, and ethical conduct. Foster a culture of continuous learning and collaborative knowledge sharing to boost overall research productivity and quality.
